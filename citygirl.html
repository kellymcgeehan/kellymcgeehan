city girl

poetry

new years eve. pours talk

as boy walks (in. his eyes)

the room glimmers haunt.

why is he here,

asks girl.

it's his girlfriend's party, friends say

hey, don't get bent out of shape.

the room is ringing.

i know. he knows i know. i said no,

thinks girl.

but no point in telling.

me, the girl with no proof. that no

(in his eyes) means promise.

at midnight the room toasts

glasses half empty and

he looks at girl. no, through

her. like he did in an upstairs

frat bed while she gave

cold shoulder. ringing

in the new year.

girl hopes he gets a taste

of his own skin.

Annotations

The piece selected here is a unpublished poem titled “city girl” that I wrote for my poetry class. I am showcasing this piece because I want my portfolio to communicate that I am a promising creative writer, and I believe my poetry best exemplifies this potential.
