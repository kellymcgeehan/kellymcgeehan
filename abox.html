a box labeled: box

poetry

There was a manhunt for a serial assaulter in South Philadelphia. He attacked

nine women, on both sides of Broad, biking around night and day, rounding street corners, past our homes, peering about, I bet, for prey.

I watched the reports obsessively. Because two occurred along my running route. Because I’m an assault survivor.

Because the word “serial” makes ears perk. Makes news. Makes a tragedy a Netflix trend.

“Serial” means repeated behavioral patterns.

To repeatedly intrude a home, a body, and leave unscathed leads an intruder to believe the past is behind them. But memories cannot be boxed.

In the time a therapist calls my “pre-trauma life” I was in love with a boy, stationed states away. We wrote letters now stored under my bed in a box labeled: BOX

OF BOYFRIENDS PAST. His are its only artifacts. A shrine I don’t often honor.

It’s not that I resent public media. It’s that my home is too loud. My home is invaded by satellite.

On visits we (boy and I) kept a journal: On Hollywood Boulevard I got a stomach ache. In June he said he’d missed me. Then backed into a parked car in Little Tokyo. I remember

the thump. of trains heading west on Regional Rail. in the days we dreamed of living in “the city.”

“the” city meaning Philadelphia. “The” is commonly used to refer to a place or thing that is unique.

For example, the city I dreamed of and the Philadelphia I live in are not the same cities.

The past still does not taste as the future promised.

Annotations

The piece selected here is a unpublished poem titled “a box labeled: box” that I wrote for my poetry class. I am showcasing this piece because I want my portfolio to communicate that I am a promising creative writer, and I believe my poetry best exemplifies this potential.
