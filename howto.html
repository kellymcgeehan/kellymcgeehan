how to hold a mom

poetry

A mom’s voice is just out of reach.

so Hold her tightly in momentos. She always wanted a daughter.

That's what bubble letters in a scrapbook from ‘97 declare:

"FINALLY, A GIRL!" Older brothers

are mere dog ears in her autobiography. She always

meaning without exception. wanted you. Most of the time

a mom promises to be better. Then calls to inform

she's not. Coming to Christmas brunch.

Don't ask why. Sweep it under the rug. Get used to staying at dad's.

In the mirror. Hold a mom here.

A clone of heart shaped face and mousy hair

reflects back. Except her meadow eyes

are left in drought. In yours. reflecting hazel. Your dad's.

Of course no girl wants to look "just like her father."

If only you knew. What a mom looks like.

Because when you hold a mom. with white knuckles.

lying limp on the yellow tile floor

you don't look at her. Don't look at her. Don't look at me,

a mom will tell you.

Concentrate instead on the yellow tile. Try to sweep this under the rug.

Notice there isn't one.

Annotations

The piece selected here is a unpublished poem titled “how to hold a mom” that I wrote for my poetry class. I am showcasing this piece because I want my portfolio to communicate that I am a promising creative writer, and I believe my poetry best exemplifies this potential.
